# Proposal Writing

## Workflow

1. Identify client, problem, desired outcome, evaluation criteria, and deadline.
2. Structure the proposal around need, approach, deliverables, timeline, responsibilities, assumptions, and risks.
3. Make scope and exclusions explicit.
4. Tie benefits to the client's stated goals.
5. Keep commitments realistic and review-sensitive terms visible.

## Style

- Be specific about deliverables and acceptance.
- Avoid vague promises and unsupported ROI claims.
- Use tables for timeline, pricing, assumptions, and responsibilities.

## Verification

- Check names, dates, scope, dependencies, and commercial terms.
- Flag legal, procurement, or finance review needs.
