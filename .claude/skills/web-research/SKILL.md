# Web Research

## Workflow

1. Clarify the research question, decision context, geography, date sensitivity, and required confidence.
2. Prefer primary or official sources when accuracy, policy, pricing, legal, medical, financial, or technical details matter.
3. Track source title, URL, publisher, publication date, effective date, and access date when useful.
4. Compare sources for agreement, conflict, freshness, and missing context.
5. Summarize into the user's requested decision format.

## Rules

- Verify unstable facts with current sources.
- Do not treat marketing copy as neutral evidence.
- Separate sourced facts, source claims, analysis, and recommendations.
- Mark uncertainty and source limitations.

## Verification

- Include source links when sources were used.
- Check that conclusions are supported by cited evidence.
- Flag what would change the answer.
